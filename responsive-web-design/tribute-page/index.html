Dennis Ritchie

Creator of the C programming language

Timeline of Ritchie's Life

  1. 1941: Born in Bronxville, New York
  2. 1963: Earns a bachelor's degree in physics from Harvard University
  3. 1967: Joins Bell Labs to start working on the Multics operating system, following in the footsteps of his father Alistair E. Ritchie who worked as a switching systems engineer at Bell Labs.
  4. 1968: Earns a doctorate degree in mathematics from Harvard University
  5. 1972: The first version of the C programming language is published
  6. 1973: Ritchie and Thompson rewrite the Unix operating system in C
  7. 1978: Publishes The C Programming Language with Brian Kernighan
  8. 1983: Awarded the Turing Award by the ACM
  9. 1990: Awarded the Hamming Medal by the IEEE
  10. 1999: Recieves the National Medal of Technology from President Bill Clinton
  11. 2011: Dies at the age of 70
"There's that line from Newton about standing on the shoulders of giants. We're all standing on Dennis' shoulders."

- Brian Kernighan
